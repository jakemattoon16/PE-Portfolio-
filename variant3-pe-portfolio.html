The Structural Problem

The Reporting Lag That Destroys Exit Multiples

A PE-backed business has a 3-to-7-year holding period. Every quarter of underperformance against the investment model compounds the gap between current trajectory and exit multiple target. The reporting infrastructure at most portfolio companies is not built to close that gap.

30 days

Covenant Blind Window

Lenders test covenants at month-end. A company drifting toward a leverage breach has a 30-day blind spot before the breach is formally identified. A 3.9x ratio against a 4.0x hard limit needs a daily signal, not a monthly one.

45 days

EBITDA Variance Discovery Lag

A $2M EBITDA bridge variance that takes 45 days to surface is a variance that has been compounding for 45 days. Leadership cannot mount a corrective response when the signal arrives this late. They can only explain the miss in retrospect.

4 logins

Per-Company ERP Overhead

An Operating Partner with four portfolio companies is logging into four ERP environments, running four different export sequences, and reviewing four different report formats to compile a picture that should already exist as a unified daily briefing.

The Covenant Breach That Was Entirely Predictable

Most covenant breaches in PE portfolio companies are not sudden. They are the terminal outcome of a trend that was visible in the operational data for 30 to 60 days before the breach date. A company running at 3.8x leverage in month one, 3.85x in month two, and 3.9x in month three does not need to wait for month four to discover it is breaching at 4.05x.

Corelytics Automated Risk Detection monitors every tracked ratio on a daily basis. When a metric crosses a predefined warning threshold, an alert fires to the Operating Partner and portfolio CFO. The corrective window is measured in weeks, not discovered in a lender call.
